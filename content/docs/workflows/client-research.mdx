## Cold Outreach That Doesn't Feel Cold

The worst business emails all sound the same: "I came across your company and thought there might be synergies..." Delete.

The best ones sound like the person actually did their homework: they know your situation, they reference something specific, and their offer feels relevant — not random.

The difference isn't talent. It's preparation. And preparation takes time — time most people skip because they have 15 other things to do.

This workflow does the preparation for you. One command. Real research. Personalized outreach. In your voice.

1. Google the person and their company — open 8 tabs
2. Check LinkedIn — read their recent posts, scan their bio
3. Visit the company website — figure out what they actually do
4. Think about how your services connect to their situation
5. Draft an email — rewrite it three times to not sound generic
6. Think about follow-up timing — then forget to follow up

**Time:** 30-45 minutes per prospect. You do maybe 3-4 a week before burning out.

- **Follow-up sequence** — 2-3 follow-ups with suggested timing

**Time:** About 3 minutes. You review and edit the output, then send.

## The Command Template

<Tabs items={["Template", "Complete Example"]}>
<Tab>

Save this as `.claude/commands/research-client.md`. This is the most advanced command in the course — it uses agents, sub-agents, and your full context stack:

````
You are my client research and outreach assistant.

Research this prospect: $ARGUMENTS

Read these files for context:
- @identity.md — who I am, my expertise, my services
- @company.md — my business, ideal client profile, current offerings
- @style-guide.md — how I write and communicate

Check prospects/ and clients/ folders for any existing
information about this person or their company.

## Step 1: Research
Create two agents to research in parallel:

Agent 1 — Company Research:
Analyze any files I have about this company. Summarize what
they do, their size, their market, and any recent changes
or challenges they might be facing.

Agent 2 — Person Research:
Analyze any files I have about this person. Their role,
background, how long they've been in the position, and
anything that suggests what they care about professionally.

## Step 2: Strategic Angle
Based on the research, identify:
- The strongest connection between what I offer and what they need
- One specific problem I could help them solve
- Why now might be the right time to reach out

## Step 3: Draft Outreach
Write a personalized email that:
- References something specific about them (not generic flattery)
- Connects their situation to my expertise in one sentence
- Makes a clear, low-pressure ask (coffee chat, quick call, etc.)
- Sounds like me — use @style-guide.md for tone and voice
- Is under 150 words — busy people don't read long cold emails

## Step 4: Follow-up Sequence
Draft 2-3 follow-up emails:
- Follow-up 1: 5-7 days later, add one new insight
- Follow-up 2: 10-14 days later, different angle or offer
- Follow-up 3 (optional): 30 days later, brief check-in

## Step 5: Editor Review
Run a sub-agent as an Editor who reviews the outreach draft
and follow-ups for:
- Anything that sounds generic or templated
- Claims I can't back up
- Tone that doesn't match my style guide
- Anything that feels pushy or salesy

Flag issues and suggest fixes.

Output everything in clean markdown with clear section headers.
````

## Making Your Research Better Over Time

Just like meeting prep, client research gets better the more information you give it to work with.

<Steps>
<Step>

**Save your research.** After running `/research-client`, save the output to a `prospects/` folder. Use the person's name as the filename: `prospects/maria-torres.md`.

</Step>
<Step>

**After a call, update the file.** Add notes about what you discussed, what they're interested in, and any next steps.

</Step>
<Step>

**When they become a client, move the file.** Move it from `prospects/` to `clients/`. Now your `/prep-meeting` command can find it too.

</Step>
<Step>

**The workflows compound.** `/research-client` creates the prospect file. `/prep-meeting` reads it before your next call. Every interaction builds on the last.

</Step>
</Steps>

This is the real power of workflows: they don't just save time individually — they feed into each other.

## When Not to Use This

<Callout type="warn">
**Important limitations to keep in mind:**

- **Claude can't browse the web live.** If you want Claude to analyze someone's LinkedIn profile or company website, paste the content into a file first (e.g., `prospects/maria-torres-linkedin.txt`). Claude reads files brilliantly — it just can't visit URLs on its own.
- **Always read before you send.** The outreach draft is a starting point, not a finished product. Read every email before sending. Add personal touches, fix anything that feels off, and make sure the tone is right.
- **Don't trust claims you can't verify.** If Claude says "their company recently raised a Series B," confirm it. Claude is working from the files you gave it — it can make reasonable inferences, but it can't fact-check against the live internet.
</Callout>

7. Review the output — edit the email draft, then consider actually sending it
